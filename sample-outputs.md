2. **Dining** — Platinum's $400 Resy credit and Freedom Unlimited's 3% dining rate both target restaurant spending (credit vs. earn rate)
3. **Rideshare** — Platinum provides $200 Uber Cash and $120 Uber One credit; Blue Cash Preferred earns 3% on transit/rideshare; three cards touching the same category
4. **Purchase protection** — All three cards offer purchase protection and extended warranty coverage

## 🕳️ Gaps
1. **Hotels booked outside Amex Travel** — No bonus earning on direct hotel bookings; falls to 1–1.5x
2. **Home improvement / hardware stores** — No bonus category; earns only 1–1.5%
3. **Online shopping (non-streaming)** — No dedicated multiplier for general e-commerce
4. **Cell phone / wireless bill** — No bonus rate or cell phone protection on any of the three cards
5. **Utilities** — No bonus earning on electric, water, or internet bills
6. **International spending** — Freedom Unlimited charges 3% FTF; Blue Cash Preferred charges 2.7% FTF; only Amex Platinum has no FTF but Amex acceptance is weaker abroad

## 📋 Confidence Notes
- Amex Platinum $895 annual fee took effect for renewals on or after January 2, 2026; if your renewal hasn't passed, you may still be on the prior $695 fee
- Blue Cash Preferred's $120 Disney Bundle credit may overlap with the Platinum's $300 digital entertainment credit if covering the same streaming services — confirm which subscriptions are enrolled on which card
- Chase Freedom Unlimited's DoorDash quarterly credit and DashPass benefit expire December 31, 2027
- Amex Platinum credits are use-it-or-lose-it on monthly/quarterly/semi-annual cycles — missing a period forfeits that portion
